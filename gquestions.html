User Design

What is design?

Design in graphics is defined as a craft where graphic designers create visual content in order to relay messages. Designers use typography to meet users’ need.

What is user design?

User design is the process designers use to create products. They try to provide meaningful and relevant experiences to users. This process involves acquiring and integrating the product,includingthe aspects of branding, designing, and function.

Prototyping

What is Prototyping?

A prototype is an early sample, model or release of a product created to test a concept or process.Typically, a prototype is used to evaluate a new design to improve the accuracy of analysts and system users. It is the step between the formalization and the evaluation of an idea. Prototypesare a crucial part of the design processand a practice used in all design disciplines. From architects, engineers, industrial designers and even service designers, they make their prototypes to test their designs before investing in their mass production.(Ramirez, 2018)

What is the difference between low-fidelity and high-fidelity prototypes?

Low-fidelity prototypes are usually drawn on paper and does not allow any user interactions. Low fidelity prototypes are easier and quicker to create. They help developers by giving them an early visualization of design solutions.High-Fidelity prototypes are computerized and allows user interaction.

Why is prototyping important?

Having a prototype makes it easier to understand the tasks for each team member. It gives them the opportunity to do more realistic planning of development deadlines and more accurately determine the cost of work. Website development in strict accordance with the prototype allows not taking into account the cost of changes and modifications.(Mishra, 2019)
